# PRODUCTS
## Describing Products Through Transformation, Not Features

**Purpose:** Framework for describing what we offer through the lens of transformation  
**Owner:** Product & Marketing  
**Version:** 1.0 (Architecture)  
**Status:** Framework Ready - Content Pending  

---

## OBJECTIVE

This document describes products NOT by features or specifications, but by the transformation they enable. It answers: "What changes in my life because of this product?"

---

## PRODUCT FRAMEWORK

Each product should be described with:

### 1. PRODUCT IDENTITY
- Name
- Category
- For whom (primary profile)
- Why it exists

### 2. THE BEFORE STATE
**What is the customer's situation without this product?**

- Daily reality
- Challenges faced
- Time spent on workarounds
- Costs (time, money, stress)
- Limitations felt
- Workarounds used

### 3. THE TRANSFORMATION
**What changes because of this product?**

- How the day looks different
- What becomes easier
- What becomes possible
- What is no longer a constraint
- New capabilities
- New opportunities

### 4. THE AFTER STATE
**What is the customer's situation with this product?**

- Daily reality (new)
- Problems solved
- Time freed
- Cost savings
- New possibilities
- Confidence gained

### 5. OUTCOME METRICS
**How does the customer measure success?**

- What they notice first
- What they track
- Realistic expectations
- Timeline for results
- What matters most

### 6. IDEAL CUSTOMER
**Who benefits most?**

- Profile/persona
- Situation they're in
- What they need
- What success means to them
- Why they would choose this

### PRODUCT 1: [Virtual Office]

#### Before
```
Independent lawyer working solo
- Building client practice through networking (slow)
- No administrative support (they handle everything)
- Irregular income (feast/famine cycle)
- Difficult to appear professional (no office)
- Spending 40% of time on non-legal work
- Can't specialize (must take all cases)
```

#### Transformation
```
Clients finding them proactively
- Client pipeline becoming steady
- Administrative tasks delegated
- Income becoming predictable
- Professional image established
- Time freed for legal work
- Can be selective about cases
- Can specialize in their expertise
```

#### After
```
Sustainable professional practice
- 3-5 qualified leads per month
- Team handling admin (they focus on law)
- Predictable monthly income
- Professional presence (office address, etc.)
- 80% of time on legal work
- Specialized practice (their focus areas)
- Growing steadily
```

#### Outcomes
```
Week 1: "Wow, people found me through the platform"
Month 1: "First qualified lead came through"
Month 3: "I can be selective about clients"
Month 6: "This is paying for itself"
Month 12: "My practice is real, not just a side gig"

Metrics they track:
- Inbound leads per month
- Time spent on business development
- New client acquisition cost
- Average case value
- Time available for specialization
- Income predictability
```

#### Best For
```
Profile: Experienced lawyer ready to scale
Situation: Currently solo practice, good expertise
Success: Sustainable growth, specialization
Why: Only platform designed for independent lawyers
```

## HOW DARWIN USES THIS

**When customer asks: "What is [product]?"**

Darwin does NOT say: "[Product] has features X, Y, Z"

Darwin SAYS: "A [Product] is for someone who [situation]. Without it, you'd be [before]. With it, [transformation happens]. Most people notice [first benefit] in the first week."

---

## LANGUAGE GUIDANCE

❌ AVOID:
- "Features include..."
- "Capability to..."
- "Functionality for..."
- "System allows..."
- Technical jargon

✅ USE:
- "Before / After"
- "What changes..."
- "No longer need to..."
- "Now you can..."
- Customer language
