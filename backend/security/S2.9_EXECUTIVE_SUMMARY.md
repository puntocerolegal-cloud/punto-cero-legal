## 🎯 The Problem

**S2.8 autonomous response was powerful but uncontrolled.**

Potential issues:
- Could isolate too many users at once (cascading failure)
- Could lock multiple tenants simultaneously
- No upper limits on autonomous actions
- No continuous validation of actual defense capability
- Risk of system self-destroying due to false positives

---

## 💡 The Solution: S2.9 — Controlled Autonomy

**Three essential functions:**

### 1. Governance Layer
Validate and limit S2.8 autonomous actions in real-time.

**Example:**
- S2.8 wants to isolate 15 users in one minute
- Governor says: "Max is 10 per minute"
- Action downgraded from ISOLATE_USER → BLOCK_USER

### 2. Safety Circuit Breakers
Auto-detect when system is in danger and degrade gracefully.

**Example:**
- 6% of users blocked (exceeds 5% threshold)
- Circuit breaker activates
- S2.8 switches to MONITOR_ONLY mode
- Prevents cascading failures

### 3. Continuous Red Team Testing
Simulate attacks in background to validate defenses.

**Example:**
- Red team injects synthetic IDOR attack
- System detects 87% (vs expected 85%)
- Effectiveness validated
- Identifies any weak points
