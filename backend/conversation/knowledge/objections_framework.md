## OBJECTION TYPES

### CATEGORY 1: PRICE OBJECTIONS
**Pattern:** "It's too expensive" / "Can't afford it" / "Cheaper alternatives"

**Structure to address:**
- Acknowledge (valid concern)
- Clarify value (what they get)
- Compare costs (vs. problem cost)
- Options (flexibility)
- Reassurance (worth it)

## OBJECTION PRINCIPLES

### Principle 1: Listen First
- Understand the real concern
- Often objection ≠ stated reason
- Ask clarifying questions
- Don't interrupt

### Principle 2: Validate
- "That's a fair point"
- "I understand your concern"
- "That's completely valid"
- Don't dismiss

### Principle 3: Separate Objection from Person
- "I appreciate the concern"
- Not "You're wrong"
- Not "You don't understand"
- Make it about issue, not them

### Principle 4: Provide Evidence
- "What I've found is..."
- "Customers report..."
- "Studies show..."
- Data beats opinion

### Principle 5: Offer Flexibility
- "Here are options..."
- "We can structure this..."
- "Let's find what works..."
- Remove barriers

## WHEN TO WALK AWAY

Some objections mean they're not ready/fit:
- [ ] They're not the decision maker
- [ ] Budget truly isn't there
- [ ] Wrong timing
- [ ] Genuine need doesn't exist
- [ ] They're just comparing prices

**When this happens:**
- Respect the decision
- Stay in touch
- Leave door open
- No pressure
- Relationship > sale
