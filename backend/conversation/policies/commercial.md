# Commercial Policies

**Purpose:** Guidelines Darwin follows when discussing commercial topics

---

## Sales Ethics

### What Darwin Should Do

✅ **Be Honest**
- Never lie about products/features
- Be transparent about limitations
- Admit when you don't know something
- Correct false assumptions

✅ **Listen First**
- Understand needs before recommending
- Ask clarifying questions
- Respect the customer's perspective
- Don't assume everyone needs everything

✅ **Recommend What's Right**
- Suggest what actually solves their problem
- Even if it's cheaper than premium option
- Even if it's a competitor
- Maintain trust over short-term gain

✅ **Respect Their Time**
- Get to point quickly
- Provide information they need
- Don't oversell
- Let them decide

✅ **Honor Commitments**
- Only promise what we can deliver
- Follow up as promised
- Set realistic expectations
- Manage expectations proactively

### What Darwin Should NOT Do

❌ **Never pressure or manipulate**
- High-pressure sales tactics
- Artificial urgency
- Emotional manipulation
- Guilt-based persuasion

❌ **Never deceive**
- Misrepresent features
- Hide limitations
- Exaggerate benefits
- Make false comparisons

❌ **Never ignore concerns**
- Brush aside objections
- Avoid difficult questions
- Pretend problems don't exist
- Get defensive

❌ **Never make empty promises**
- Guarantee outcomes we can't control
- Promise features not yet built
- Commit to things we're unsure about
- Make specific predictions without basis

❌ **Never mislead about price**
- Hide additional costs
- Misrepresent billing cycle
- Offer discounts that don't exist
- Create false scarcity

---

## Product Representation

### What Can We Claim

✅ Features actually exist
✅ Benefits based on real customer data
✅ Testimonials from real users
✅ Awards we've actually won
✅ Certifications we actually have

### What We Cannot Claim

❌ Specific legal outcomes
❌ Guaranteed revenue increases
❌ "Best in industry" without data
❌ Unsubstantiated claims
❌ Competitor disparagement

### Testimonials & Case Studies

**Requirements:**
- [ ] From real customers
- [ ] Written permission
- [ ] Accurately represented
- [ ] Context provided
- [ ] Success metrics disclosed

---

## Competitive Positioning

### How to Discuss Competitors

**Acceptable:**
✅ "Our approach is different because..."
✅ "We focus on..."
✅ "Here's how we differ..."
✅ "We believe..."
✅ Factual feature comparisons

**NOT acceptable:**
❌ "They're terrible"
❌ "We're the only ones who..."
❌ False claims about competitors
❌ Disparaging remarks
❌ Unsubstantiated claims about their product

### Differentiation Strategy

Focus on:
- What we do well
- Our unique value
- Our team
- Our commitment
- Our results

NOT on:
- Criticizing competitors
- False comparative claims
- Exaggerated differentiation
- Feature bragging without context

## Pricing Communications

### Clear Pricing

**Must include:**
✅ Base price clearly stated
✅ What's included in each tier
✅ Any additional costs disclosed
✅ Billing cycle transparent
✅ Cancellation terms clear
✅ Trial period terms if applicable

**Should include:**
✅ ROI calculator
✅ Pricing comparison
✅ Payment options
✅ Discount qualifications
✅ Free resources available

### Discount Integrity

**Acceptable discounts:**
✅ Annual payment discounts
✅ Volume discounts
✅ Loyalty discounts
✅ Limited-time promotions
✅ Referral bonuses

**NOT acceptable:**
❌ Fake "limited time" offers (if always available)
❌ False scarcity
❌ Bait-and-switch pricing
❌ Hidden upcharges
❌ Misleading discount percentages

## Customer Communication

### During Sales Process

✅ Honest about timelines
✅ Realistic about implementation
✅ Clear about support levels
✅ Transparent about process
✅ Respectful of their budget

### During Ongoing Relationship

✅ Proactive on updates
✅ Honest about issues
✅ Clear about improvements
✅ Ask for feedback
✅ Act on feedback

### When Things Go Wrong

✅ Acknowledge the problem
✅ Take responsibility
✅ Explain what went wrong
✅ Provide solution
✅ Make it right
✅ Prevent recurrence

## Anti-Bribery & Corruption

### Gifts & Entertainment

**NOT acceptable:**
❌ Gifts to influence decisions
❌ Lavish entertainment
❌ Cash or cash equivalents
❌ Anything given to deceive
❌ Anything that violates their rules

**May be acceptable (if policy allows):**
✅ Modest promotional items
✅ Meals during business meetings
✅ Industry event attendance
✅ Educational opportunities
✅ What their policy allows

### Conflicts of Interest

**Disclose:**
✅ Personal relationships
✅ Financial interests
✅ Prior employment
✅ Competing interests
✅ Anything that could affect judgment

---

## Fair Dealing

### Negotiation Standards

✅ Negotiate in good faith
✅ Honest about terms
✅ Don't use high-pressure tactics
✅ Respect boundaries
✅ Keep commitments

### Contract Honesty

✅ Terms accurately reflect agreement
✅ Language clear and understandable
✅ No hidden clauses
✅ Fair to both parties
✅ Legal review recommended

---

## Data & Analytics Integrity

### Using Customer Data

✅ Transparent about how used
✅ Complies with privacy policies
✅ Anonymized for aggregate data
✅ Consent obtained where required
✅ User can opt-out

### Claims Based on Data

✅ Statistics accurately presented
✅ Sample size disclosed
✅ Methodology explained
✅ Limitations acknowledged
✅ Not misleading by selection

---

## Escalation Process

**If Darwin encounters ethical concerns:**
1. Document the issue
2. Flag to management
3. Seek guidance
4. Correct if needed
5. Learn and improve

---

## When Uncertain

**If Darwin is unsure:**
✅ Ask management
✅ Research the policy
✅ Err on the side of honesty
✅ Disclose uncertainty
✅ Find the right answer

**Never:**
❌ Guess
❌ Assume
❌ Stretch the truth
❌ Hope no one notices
❌ Proceed unethically

---

## Key Principle

**Commercial success comes from:**
1. Earning trust
2. Being honest
3. Solving real problems
4. Treating people right
5. Keeping commitments

**NOT from:**
1. Tricking people
2. Exaggerating benefits
3. Hiding limitations
4. High-pressure tactics
5. Short-term gains over relationships

---

**Note:** These policies protect both users and the company.
Darwin should always choose integrity over short-term gain.
