# SALES PLAYBOOK - CONSULTIVE SELLING
## How to Diagnose, Understand, and Recommend

**Purpose:** Framework for consultive selling approach  
**Owner:** Sales & Business Development  
**Version:** 1.0 (Architecture)  
**Status:** Framework Ready - Content Pending  

---

## OBJECTIVE

This playbook codifies HOW we sell. Not WHAT we sell, but HOW we approach the selling conversation. The philosophy: Help first, sell second.

**Core Principle:** We don't sell. We diagnose. Then we recommend. Selling is the natural result of genuine help.

---

## THE SALES PHILOSOPHY

### NOT Transactional
- Not about closing a deal
- Not about getting a commitment
- Not about one conversation
- Not about commission

### BUT Consultive
- About solving a problem
- About building trust
- About creating a relationship
- About being genuinely helpful

### The Paradox
The best salespeople don't try to sell. They try to help. And the sale follows naturally.

## SALES FLOW ARCHITECTURE

### PHASE 1: DIAGNOSIS

**Goal:** Understand the customer's actual situation

**Approach:**
- Listen more than speak
- Ask questions that matter
- Understand their perspective
- Avoid assumptions
- See the full picture

**Questions to ask:**

**About their situation:**
- "Tell me about your current situation"
- "What's working well for you?"
- "What's not working?"
- "How long has this been an issue?"
- "What have you tried to solve it?"

**About the impact:**
- "How does this affect you personally?"
- "How does it impact your business?"
- "What's the cost of the status quo?"
- "What would change if this was solved?"

**About their goals:**
- "What's your ideal outcome?"
- "What does success look like?"
- "What would you be able to do differently?"
- "Why is this important now?"

**About their constraints:**
- "What's preventing you from solving this?"
- "What have you tried?"
- "What concerns do you have?"
- "What would make you hesitate?"

**Key skills:**
- Active listening
- Clarifying questions
- Empathy
- Curiosity (genuine)
- Not jumping to solutions

**Red flags to watch:**
- Customer still doesn't understand their own problem
- You're still assuming, not understanding
- Customer hasn't expressed urgency
- Customer is only thinking about cost

**Success criteria:**
- Customer feels heard
- You understand their situation
- They understand their problem better
- They've acknowledged urgency
- Trust is building

---

### PHASE 2: EDUCATION

**Goal:** Help them think differently about the problem

**Approach:**
- Share relevant perspective
- Show patterns you've seen
- Introduce new way of thinking
- Don't pitch your solution yet
- Make them smarter about the problem

**How to educate:**

**Share patterns:**
- "I see this pattern often..."
- "Typically what happens is..."
- "The root cause is usually..."
- "Most people miss the fact that..."

**Show possibilities:**
- "What if you could..."
- "Imagine if you were able to..."
- "What would change if..."
- "Many organizations are now..."

**Reframe thinking:**
- "Another way to look at this..."
- "The real opportunity might be..."
- "Instead of [old way], consider [new way]..."
- "Here's what we're seeing in the market..."

**Types of education:**

**Market perspective:**
- "The industry is shifting toward..."
- "Trends show..."
- "Competitors are..."
- "Best practices are evolving to..."

**Solution perspective:**
- "There are generally three approaches..."
- "Different organizations handle this by..."
- "The most effective way is..."
- "What works depends on..."

**Implementation perspective:**
- "Organizations typically take X approach..."
- "The timeline usually looks like..."
- "Key success factors are..."
- "Common mistakes include..."

**Success criteria:**
- Customer has new perspective
- They understand their options
- They see why this matters
- They're thinking bigger
- They recognize the opportunity

---

### PHASE 3: ORIENTATION

**Goal:** Help them see how your solution fits

**Approach:**
- Connect their situation to solution
- Show how transformation happens
- Explain the approach
- Address concerns directly
- Make it concrete

**How to orient:**

**Connect the dots:**
- "Based on what you've shared, here's what I'd recommend..."
- "Your situation suggests..."
- "For someone in your position..."
- "Given your constraints..."

**Explain the approach:**
- "Here's how we'd handle this..."
- "The approach would be..."
- "This is what the journey looks like..."
- "We'd start with..."

**Paint the picture:**
- "In 3 months, you'd have..."
- "After implementation, you'd be able to..."
- "The transformation would look like..."
- "You'd go from [before] to [after]..."

**Address concerns:**
- "I hear the concern about [X]. Here's why..."
- "That's a fair point. The reality is..."
- "Most people worry about that. What happens is..."
- "That used to be true. Now..."

**Success criteria:**
- Customer sees how solution fits
- They can imagine the transformation
- Concerns are addressed
- They see the value
- They're ready to decide

---

### PHASE 4: RECOMMENDATION

**Goal:** Make a clear recommendation

**Approach:**
- Be specific
- Be honest
- Be confident
- Connect to their goals
- Make it easy to say yes

**How to recommend:**

**Be specific:**
- "I recommend [specific product/approach]"
- "Here's why: [reasons]"
- "For your situation, this makes sense because..."
- "This is the best fit because..."

**Be honest:**
- "This will require..."
- "You should expect..."
- "The timeline is..."
- "The investment is..."
- "This won't solve..."

**Be confident:**
- "This is what I'd do"
- "This is the right move"
- "I've seen this work many times"
- "This is your best path"

**Make it actionable:**
- "Here's the next step..."
- "We'd start by..."
- "First, we'd need..."
- "Let me send you..."

**Success criteria:**
- Recommendation is clear
- Customer understands why
- Customer can make a decision
- Next steps are clear
- Buying seems natural

---

### PHASE 5: DECISION

**Goal:** Help them decide

**Approach:**
- Remove obstacles
- Make it easy
- Respect their pace
- Be available
- Follow up

**What to do:**

**If YES:**
- Celebrate (genuine excitement)
- Explain next steps
- Set expectations
- Introduce team
- Start strong

**If NOT YET:**
- Understand why
- Address remaining concerns
- Offer options
- Set follow-up date
- Stay in touch

**If NO:**
- Understand why
- Respect their decision
- Learn from feedback
- Leave door open
- Maintain relationship

**Success criteria:**
- Clear decision is made
- Customer feels good about it
- Next steps are aligned
- Relationship continues

## CONSULTIVE SELLING PRINCIPLES

### 1. Listen First
- Ask before telling
- Understand before suggesting
- Respect their intelligence
- Don't interrupt

### 2. Help Without Selling
- Your job is to help them think
- Not to convince them
- Not to manipulate them
- Not to pressure them

### 3. Be Honest
- About capabilities
- About limitations
- About timelines
- About costs

### 4. Respect Their Decision
- Maybe they don't buy
- Maybe they're not ready
- Maybe they choose competitor
- Respect matters more than sale

### 5. Build Trust
- Trust is more valuable than deal
- Trust leads to repeat business
- Trust leads to referrals
- Trust is the real sale

---

## RED FLAGS

**Stop selling if:**
- Customer doesn't understand problem
- You're pushing instead of pulling
- Trust is declining
- Customer feels pressured
- You're not being honest
- Customer is being pushed by others
- Timing is clearly wrong
- Solution doesn't actually fit
